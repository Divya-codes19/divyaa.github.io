Meteors & Galaxies

Meteors, often called “shooting stars,” are small fragments of rock or dust that enter Earth’s atmosphere and burn up due to friction, creating bright streaks of light. Meteor showers occur when Earth passes through the debris left behind by comets, offering spectacular celestial displays.

Beyond meteors, galaxies are vast systems of stars, planets, gas, and dust bound together by gravity. Our own Milky Way galaxy contains billions of stars and is just one among countless galaxies in the universe. Studying galaxies helps scientists understand cosmic evolution, dark matter, and the large-scale structure of the universe.

Together, meteors and galaxies remind us of the immense scale and beauty of the cosmos, inspiring curiosity and exploration across generations.
