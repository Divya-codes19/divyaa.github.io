Comet

Comets are icy celestial bodies that orbit the Sun. As they approach, heat causes them to release gas and dust, forming glowing tails that can stretch millions of kilometers. These tails always point away from the Sun due to solar wind.

Famous comets like Halley’s Comet have been observed for centuries, inspiring myths and scientific curiosity. Their composition offers clues about the early solar system and the origins of water and organic molecules on Earth.
