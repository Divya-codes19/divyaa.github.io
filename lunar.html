Lunar Eclipse

A lunar eclipse occurs when Earth comes directly between the Sun and the Moon, casting its shadow on the Moon. Depending on alignment, eclipses can be partial, total, or penumbral. During a total lunar eclipse, the Moon often appears red, earning the nickname “Blood Moon.”

These events are visible from large portions of Earth and have fascinated civilizations for centuries. They provide scientists with opportunities to study Earth’s atmosphere and the dynamics of celestial alignments.

Observing a lunar eclipse connects us to the rhythm of the cosmos, reminding us of the delicate balance between Earth, Moon, and Sun.
